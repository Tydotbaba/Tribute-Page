Alexander Graham Bell

The great man!

Tribute Info

Early life:

He was born in Edinburgh, Scotland, on March 3, 1847. At the age of 12, Bell built a homemade device that combined rotating paddles with sets of nail brushes, creating a simple dehusking machine that was put into operation at the mill and used steadily for a number of years.

Work with the deaf:

He provided training for the Boston School for Deaf Mutes (which continues today as the public Horace Mann School for the Deaf).

Continuing experimentation:

He continued his experiments.

The telephone:

He was the original inventor of the telephone.

Death:

He died on August 2, 1922 at the age of 75 in Beinn Bhreagh, Nova Scotia, Canada.
